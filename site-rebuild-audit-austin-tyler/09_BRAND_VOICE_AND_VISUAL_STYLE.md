# 09 — Brand Voice and Visual Style

## Overall Brand Impression
A young, authentic, Nashville-rooted **music photographer/videographer** who sells himself on *realness over polish* — "capturing energy, emotion, and presence as they naturally unfold." The work is confident and genuinely good; the brand wrapper around it is unfinished. The essence: **intimate, honest, music-scene documentary** with a country/Americana lean.

## Tone of Voice
- **First person, warm, direct:** "I'm Austin Tyler…", "Tell me about your project", "I'll get back to you within 24–48 hours."
- **Values-led:** authenticity, connection, trust, storytelling.
- **Confident but not corporate** — short declaratives: "It's not posed — it's alive.", "It's branding with purpose, not just visuals.", "Where concept meets clarity."
- Slightly inconsistent in execution (mix of lowercase, ALL CAPS, and Title Case; a few typos), which undercuts the otherwise strong voice.

## Repeated Words / Phrases (the brand lexicon — keep)
`authenticity / authentic` · `energy, emotion, and presence` · `connection` · `tell stories that last` · `real / raw` · `not posed — alive` · `branding with purpose` · `concept meets clarity` · `available to tour` · `based in nashville, available for travel`.

## Personality (what the brand IS)
✅ Artistic · ✅ Raw / authentic · ✅ Music-focused · ✅ Photography- & video-focused · ✅ Dark/moody · ✅ Local (Nashville) · ✅ Country/Americana-leaning · ✅ Personal/solo-creator.
❌ NOT: corporate, premium-luxury, playful/quirky, minimal-clinical, mass-market.

## Visual Style
- **Colors:** predominantly black/white/grayscale (lots of B&W live shots) with bursts of saturated **stage lighting** (orange, blue, purple, pink) and warm promo-cover tones. Effective palette for the new site: near-black background, off-white text, one accent pulled from stage lighting (a warm amber or a deep red would suit the country/honky-tonk vibe).
- **Typography (current):** Squarespace template defaults; the wordmark "austintylerartistry." is rendered lowercase with a trailing period as the signature device. Casing is inconsistent elsewhere. *Recommendation:* pick ONE system — a clean modern sans for UI + a characterful display face for headlines; standardize casing (lean into the lowercase wordmark as a motif, but be consistent).
- **Image style:** documentary, available-light, lots of B&W; live-performance motion + intimate studio/outdoor portraits; square promo single covers with title typography. **This is the brand.** Let images dominate.
- **Layout style:** image-stacked sections (Squarespace). Currently a bit flat/listy; needs stronger hierarchy and whitespace.
- **Spacing:** uneven (uses a blank spacer image instead of real spacing).
- **Motion/animation:** minimal; vertical video clips are the main motion. Opportunity for subtle reveals + a hero reel.
- **Texture/mood:** gritty, low-light, real-venue atmosphere. Honky-tonk neon, stage haze, Bluebird Cafe.
- **Recurring motifs:** the lowercase "austintylerartistry." wordmark with trailing period; stage spotlights/rays; B&W performance; square cover art.

## What Should Be PRESERVED
- The first-person, authenticity-driven voice and the brand lexicon above.
- The "austintylerartistry." lowercase-with-period wordmark.
- B&W-heavy, documentary, music-forward imagery.
- "available to tour" / "based in Nashville, available for travel."
- The three-collection framing (Live / Promotional / Studio).

## What Should Be UPDATED
- Standardize **capitalization** and fix typos.
- Establish a real **type system** and consistent spacing (drop the spacer image).
- Stronger **visual hierarchy** (hero, section rhythm, CTAs).
- Pick a deliberate **accent color** from the stage-light palette.
- Replace logo-as-OG-image with real photos.

## What Should Be REMOVED
- All template/demo/placeholder content (Ecosystem, "Store 2", "Our Story", "Your Site Title").
- Stock/demo images; the "Brian Russell Collins Invoice.png" spacer.
- Duplicate pages.

## Suggested New Direction (one paragraph)
Keep it **dark, cinematic, and image-led** — a near-black canvas where Austin's photography and a horizontal hero reel carry the page, with one warm stage-light accent and a clean, consistent type system. Lean into the lowercase "austintylerartistry." signature and the documentary B&W aesthetic, but tighten everything around it: a real headline + Book CTA, organized Live/Promo/Studio collections, a genuine About with his face and story, named artist work as proof, and a single clear booking path. Same soul — authentic Nashville music storyteller — just finished, consistent, and built to convert.
