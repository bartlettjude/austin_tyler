# Copywriting Observations

## What's working (keep the voice)
- **First-person, warm, authentic.** "I'm Austin Tyler…", "Tell me about your project", "I'll get back to you within 24–48 hours."
- **Strong, values-led brand copy.** The bio and the three collection paragraphs are genuinely good — punchy closers like *"It's not posed — it's alive."*, *"branding with purpose, not just visuals."*, *"where concept meets clarity."*
- **Distinctive lines:** "available to tour", "based in Nashville, available for travel."

## What needs fixing
### Typos (live on the site)
- "Music **Entertainement**…" → Entertainment (home title)
- "…care about **AUTHENITICTY**…" → AUTHENTICITY (/information)
- "EVENT **SCHEDUKED** FOR 1 HOUR…" → scheduled (store fine print)
- "…CONTENT HAS BEEN **AQUIRED**." → acquired (store fine print)
- "**tell about your project**" → tell **me** about your project (/contact)

### Consistency
- Capitalization is all over the place: lowercase headings, ALL-CAPS bullets, "i'll" / "i'LL". Pick a system and apply sitewide. The lowercase wordmark style can be intentional, but be consistent.
- ALL-CAPS service deliverables are hard to read → sentence case.

### Gaps
- **No homepage headline** — needs a real value-prop line (the H1 is just the brand name).
- **$250 combo** package is under-described.
- **3 empty product descriptions** ("AUSTIN TYLER" placeholder).
- No FAQ, no About narrative, no testimonials, no usage/terms copy.

## Tone guide for new copy
Keep it: first-person, confident, plainspoken, music-insider, authenticity-obsessed. Short declarative closers. Avoid corporate/generic phrasing (the auto-generated SEO descriptions — "memorable moments with top photographers", "ecosystem solutions" — are the opposite of his real voice; don't let that creep back in).

## Lexicon to reuse
authenticity · energy, emotion, and presence · connection · real / raw · not posed — alive · branding with purpose · concept meets clarity · stories that last · available to tour.
