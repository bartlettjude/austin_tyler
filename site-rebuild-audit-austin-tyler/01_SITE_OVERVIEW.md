## Main purpose of the site
Win bookings from **music artists, bands, brands, and creatives** who need photo/video content — live shows, promotional/release content, and studio sessions — primarily in the Nashville scene.

## Primary audience
- Independent & emerging **musicians/artists** (heavy country/Americana/Nashville lean based on imagery — cowboy hats, Bluebird Cafe, live honky-tonk stages).
- Their **managers/brands** needing release & tour content.
- Secondary: creatives/individuals wanting portraits.

## Main conversion goal
Get the visitor to **submit the inquiry form** (the nav "Contact" → /information) or **buy/book a package** (Services / Store).

## Secondary conversion goals
- Follow on Instagram.
- Browse the photo/video portfolio (build trust).
- Email directly.

## Current vibe
Moody, authentic, music-forward. Lots of black-and-white live performance imagery + colorful promo single covers. Lowercase stylized wordmark. The *photography itself is genuinely strong and professional.*

## First impression
"This person can clearly shoot music artists well." But also: visually it leans on a stack of images with a weak, button-less hero, a placeholder-feeling title, inconsistent capitalization, and several obviously unfinished/template pages lurking. It reads as **a talented shooter on a half-finished template** rather than a polished brand.

## Current strengths
- **Excellent, varied portfolio photography** (the core asset).
- **Strong, authentic brand copy** — the bio and the three "collection" descriptions are well written and on-voice.
- Clear, fairly priced **productized packages** ($75–$250).
- Tight niche (Nashville music) and a clear human voice ("I'm Austin Tyler…").
- Real promo work for named artists (social proof potential).

## Current weaknesses
- Half-finished template: leftover "Your Site Title", "Store 2", "Our Story", "The Ecosystem" demo page, duplicate homepage, two contact pages.
- **Hero has no CTA**; **no real About page** ("About Austin" → contact form).
- **Store/booking not in the nav** — commerce is effectively hidden.
- **Broken/auto-generated SEO** (treats "Austin Tyler" as the cities "Austin and Tyler"; names a random "Sofia Parazi"; title typo "Entertainement").
- Multiple typos in live copy (AUTHENITICTY, SCHEDUKED, AQUIRED, "tell about your project").
- Stock images on 3 store products; spacer image literally named "Brian Russell Collins Invoice.png" reused ~12×.
- Inconsistent capitalization and structure throughout.

## What the site is trying to communicate
"I'm a Nashville music photographer/videographer who captures authentic, real moments — let's tell your story and I'll make content that builds connection before the music even plays."

## What the new site should PRESERVE
- The bio + three-collection copy (verbatim, lightly cleaned).
- The photography and the promo covers.
- The productized package model and pricing.
- The first-person, authentic, Nashville-music voice.
- Instagram + email as contact channels.

## What the new site should IMPROVE
- A real hero with a headline + primary **Book** CTA.
- A real **About** page.
- One clean **Contact** page (merge the two), one **Book** page (merge Services + Store).
- Fixed navigation that includes booking.
- Rewritten, correct, human SEO titles/descriptions (kill the "Austin and Tyler / Sofia Parazi" mess).
- Consistent typography/casing, fixed typos, removed template/stock/placeholder content.
- Real social proof (testimonials, named artist clients).
